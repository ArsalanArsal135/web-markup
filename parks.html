Parks

Rembrandtplaats in Leiden is a charming public square that beautifully blends nature with urban life. Located near the historic Molen de Pot windmill, this green space features lush greenery, flowerbeds, and inviting benches, making it an ideal spot for relaxation. The square is a vibrant community hub, often hosting various events and activities that create a lively atmosphere. Families can enjoy picnics while friends gather under the shade of trees. With the historic windmill adding a unique touch, Rembrandtplaats offers a serene escape in the heart of Leiden, making it a beloved destination for locals and visitors alike.

De Leidse Hout is a city park in Leiden, near the Oegstgeest border, opened on June 30, 1931, as part of a job creation project. The park is home to typical Dutch trees like oak, ash, beech, and alder, and over 140 species of mushrooms. It's the only place in Leiden where the red bat can be spotted. The park features Het Theehuis restaurant, a deer park, a bandstand, and a sports complex with tennis courts and an athletics track. The Great Playground is located on the north side, and the free Werfpop pop festival is held here annually.
